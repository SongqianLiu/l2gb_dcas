L2GB ADRS - Lane Change​
This article discusses the lane change requirements and test suggestions for the L2GB ADRS system, including general requirements, safety, turn signal control, acceleration limits, cancellation, inhibition, and different types of lane change scenarios. Key points include: ​
General requirements：The system should meet the exit and quality index requirements for lane changes, including not entering the lane change execution phase at solid lane lines, not changing lanes to oncoming lanes, maintaining continuous lateral movement, and not changing two or more lanes continuously.​
Safety requirements：Lane changes should meet the requirements of rationality and safety, including passing the field test specified in the "Lane Change Test" section, and ensuring that the deceleration of the following vehicle does not exceed 3m/s².​
Turn signal control：The turn signal should be turned on before the lane change execution process, remain on during the lane change, and be turned off or a lane change completion prompt signal should be issued after the lane change is completed.​
Acceleration limits：The lateral acceleration during the lane change execution phase should not exceed 3.5m/s², and the average value of the lateral acceleration change rate within any 0.5s should not exceed 5m/s³. The longitudinal deceleration should not exceed 2m/s² except to avoid or mitigate an imminent collision risk.​
Cancellation and inhibition：The driver can cancel the lane change at any time through a single operation. The system should not trigger a lane change if certain conditions are not met.​
Driver interaction：The driver can trigger a lane change through the turn signal lever, and a single lever turn can only perform a single lane change. The system should obtain the driver's confirmation before changing lanes, except in special circumstances.​
Test suggestions：The article provides test scenarios and passing conditions for different types of lane changes, including driver-triggered, driver-confirmed, and system-triggered lane changes, as well as vehicle target detection and response ability tests. ​
