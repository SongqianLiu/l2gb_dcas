This article discusses the requirements, system examples, and test suggestions for a system related to the recognition and response of road traffic signals. Key points include:​
System requirements : The system should be able to recognize and respond to applicable road traffic signals (e.g., vehicle traffic lights, direction indicators), and make safe responses to detected traffic signals, yield markings, stop lines, and lanes for specific traffic participants.​
Signal recognition and response : The system can recognize and respond to vehicle traffic lights, direction indicator lights, U - turn lights, and crossing lights, but does not recognize or respond to non - motor vehicle lights, pedestrian crosswalk lights, and lane lights.​
Test conditions : The test is carried out under daytime conditions on a cross - shaped intersection with at least two - way single - lane and traffic lights for vehicle recognition. The test vehicle travels at a speed of Vsmaxset, and the traffic lights are initially red. The test starts when the vehicle is at least 150m away from the intersection, and the lights turn green after the vehicle stops.​
Passing conditions : The test vehicle should pass through the intersection and cruise along the lane. ​
