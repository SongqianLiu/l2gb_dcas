# M20 — Controllability of Deceleration and Acceleration

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Risk Level** | Medium (Traceability / Validation Gap) |
| **EU Reference** | UN R.171 S2 §5.3.6.3.1 |
| **CN Reference** | GB draft §4.4.3 |
| **Gap Type** | Traceability / documentation gap |

> CN GB contains an equivalent requirement. Gap is ADRS documentation and validation evidence.

---

## EU Requirement (R.171 §5.3.6.3.1)

> The system's longitudinal control actions (deceleration and acceleration) shall remain within bounds that the driver can reasonably override, and shall not generate deceleration or acceleration that would cause the vehicle to become uncontrollable.

The core principle: system-commanded longitudinal maneuvers must always be within the controllability envelope of a typical driver.

---

## China L2GB Status

GB draft §4.4.3 states:

> 系统激活时，系统产生的减速度或加速度应在驾驶员可控范围内，不应导致车辆失控。

Translation: When the system is active, the deceleration or acceleration generated by the system shall be within the driver's controllable range and shall not cause the vehicle to lose control.

**CN and EU are aligned** — identical in principle: all system-commanded longitudinal control actions must remain within driver-controllable bounds.

---

## Required Closure Actions

1. Confirm the G1.3 ADRS references GB §4.4.3 and specifies the maximum deceleration and acceleration limits for system-commanded longitudinal control
2. Provide the specific limits: maximum commanded deceleration (m/s²), maximum commanded acceleration (m/s²), and any jerk limits
3. Confirm these limits are consistent with the comfort-related limits in §4.4.4 and §4.6.2.2.1.8
4. Validate: during system operation, verify deceleration/acceleration does not exceed the specified bounds via CAN log analysis or test track measurement
